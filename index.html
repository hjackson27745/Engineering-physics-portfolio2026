About Me

I am especially interested in projects that show the intersection between physics and engineering: fabrication, instrumentation, thermal systems, and experimental analysis. I enjoy work that requires both conceptual understanding and practical execution, whether that means building and iterating on a physical device or analyzing measurements from a real laboratory system. This portfolio highlights projects that reflect that approach.

Selected Projects

Final Fabrication Project

A hands-on fabrication project centered on designing, building, and iterating a physical system from concept to finished prototype. This project reflects my interest in translating ideas into working hardware through troubleshooting, revision, and direct engagement with materials and fabrication constraints.

Thermometry Analysis for AR Coating Autoclave

A technical analysis project related to thermometry and temperature interpretation in a small autoclave used for anti-reflection coating fabrication. This work reflects my interest in instrumentation, thermal systems, and using physics-based reasoning to better understand the behavior and limitations of real experimental equipment.
