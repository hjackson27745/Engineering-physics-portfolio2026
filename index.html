About Me

I am especially interested in projects that show the intersection between physics and engineering: fabrication, instrumentation, thermal systems, and experimental analysis. I enjoy work that requires both conceptual understanding and practical execution, whether that means building and iterating on a physical device or analyzing measurements from a real laboratory system.

This portfolio highlights projects that reflect that approach: hands-on systems, technical reasoning, and careful attention to how design choices affect performance.

Research note: I am drawn to work where physical behavior, measurement, and design constraints meet.
